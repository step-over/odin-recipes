Spaghetti Bolognese

Description

Consists of a spaghetti (long strings of pasta) served with a sauce made from tomatoes, minced beef or other meat, garlic, wine and herbs. The dish is often served with grated Parmesan on top

The sauce may be laid on top of the pasta (rather than being mixed in) or even served separately from it, leaving diners to mix it in themselves

In this recipe, we are going to teach you how to make bolognese sauce, you can buy the pasta or make it yourself

Ingredients

  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 2 cans crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
  • 1/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a roiling boil.
  2. Stir in spaghetti and return to a boil. Cook spaghetti uncovered, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, about 12 minutes; drain
  3. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, celery, carrot; wait until vegetables begin to soften, 8 to 10 minutes
  4. Add garlic; cook until fragrant, about 2 minutes
  5. Crumble in ground beef; cook and stir until no longer pink and completely cooked, 8 to 10 minutes
  6. Stir in crushed tomatoes and tomato paste; bring to a boil, season with salt and black pepper, then remove from heat
  7. Spoon sauce over spaghetti; top with Parmesan cheese to serve
